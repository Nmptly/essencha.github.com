Stranger Strings: Fans Pull the Plug as Noah Schnapp's 'Zionism Is Sexy' Video Sparks Show Boycott

In a shocking turn of events, the Stranger Things fandom is ablaze with controversy following Noah Schnapp's recent video where he declared, "Zionism is sexy." Fans are now calling for a boycott of the show, expressing their dismay on various social media platforms. Comments flooded Twitter and Instagram, with one fan stating, "I used to love Stranger Things, but I can't support a show that has actors promoting political ideologies, especially without context." Another user echoed the sentiment, adding, "Noah Schnapp needs to address this ASAP, or I'm out."

Amidst the fervor, fellow Stranger Things cast members have also been drawn into the fray. Gaten Matarazzo, who plays Dustin Henderson, responded to the controversy by urging fans to reserve judgment until Schnapp provides an explanation. Matarazzo stated, "Let's not jump to conclusions. On the other hand, Millie Bobby Brown, who portrays Eleven, remained silent on the matter, leaving fans speculating about her stance. As the boycott gains momentum, it remains to be seen how the cast and crew will navigate this unexpected storm, and whether Schnapp's explanation will be enough to quell the rising tide of discontent among fans.



The ripple effect of Schnapp's video has transcended the virtual realm, with fans organizing petitions and online campaigns to express their disappointment. Some supporters argue thatthe actor's political views should not impact the show's future, while others maintain that a boycott is necessary to hold Schnapp accountable for his statement.
